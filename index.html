About the Author

Diego Muguet de Magalhães is an independent mathematician and economist from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. His work bridges geometry, spectral dynamics, and number theory to address one of the most famous open problems in mathematics: the Riemann Hypothesis.

The Spectral-Geometric Reformulation

The central claim of this work is that the Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to the absence of torsion in a specific vector function constructed from the zeta function's non-trivial zeros:

RH ↔ τ(N) = 0 for all N > 0

The Function of Residual Oscillation, FOR(N), built from N^ρ/ρ across all zeros, behaves like a wave in the complex plane. If this wave remains directionally stable (i.e., no angular distortion), the zeros must lie on the critical line Re(ρ) = 1/2.
